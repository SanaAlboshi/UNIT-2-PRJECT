JOURNEY THROUGH THE HISTORY OF SAUDI ARABIA

1744 (1157 AH)

The First Saudi State was founded in Diriyah by Imam Muhammad bin Saud and Sheikh Muhammad bin Abdulwahhab. It became a center for unity, knowledge, and religious reform in Arabia.

1818 (1233 AH)

The First Saudi State came to an end after the Ottoman campaign led by Ibrahim Pasha, which destroyed Diriyah and marked a temporary halt in unification efforts.

1824 (1240 AH)

The Second Saudi State was founded by Imam Turki bin Abdullah, restoring stability and choosing Riyadh as the new capital.

1891 (1309 AH)

The Second Saudi State fell due to internal conflicts and external pressures, leading to the temporary loss of Riyadh.

1902 (1319 AH)

King Abdulaziz bin Abdulrahman Al Saud recaptured Riyadh, marking the beginning of the unification of the Arabian Peninsula.

1932 (1351 AH)

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia was officially established, uniting the regions under one nation and beginning an era of progress, faith, and development.
