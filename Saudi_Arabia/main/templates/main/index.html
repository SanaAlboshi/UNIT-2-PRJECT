Introduction to the History of Saudi Arabia

Origins and Beginnings

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia traces its origins to 1744 in Diriyah, when Imam Muhammad bin Saud allied with Sheikh Muhammad bin Abdulwahhab to unify the region under true Islamic principles. This was the foundation of an Arab and Islamic entity that brought unity and stability to the Arabian Peninsula.

Challenges and Perseverance

Despite the fall of the First Saudi State in 1818 by the Ottomans, the Saudi spirit endured. In 1824, Imam Turki bin Abdullah established the Second Saudi State, with Riyadh as its capital. Despite internal and external struggles, the dream of unity persisted, paving the way for the modern kingdom.

Unification and Prosperity

In 1902, King Abdulaziz bin Abdulrahman Al Saud reclaimed Riyadh, beginning the unification journey that culminated in the establishment of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia in 1932. Since then, the Kingdom has continued to grow and develop, maintaining its Islamic and Arab identity while achieving global prominence.
