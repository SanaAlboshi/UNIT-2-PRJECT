Kings of Saudi Arabia

Distinguished leadership and strategic vision have marked the history of Saudi Arabia through successive generations of the House of Saud.

King Abdulaziz (1932–1953)

Founder of modern Saudi Arabia, King Abdulaziz unified the country and established a stable state based on Quran and Sunnah. The Kingdom was officially named “Saudi Arabia” on September 23, 1932.

King Saud (1953 – 1964)

Continued his father's work in national development, internal visits across Saudi cities, and international diplomacy for cooperation and modernization.

King Faisal (1964 – 1975)

Oversaw economic and social reforms, led Saudi Arabia in international and Arab meetings, and strengthened the Kingdom’s role globally.

King Khalid (1975 – 1982)

Maintained development combining modern civilization with preservation of religious and historical constants of the Kingdom.

King Fahd (1982 – 2005)

Led comprehensive development, expanded education and infrastructure, ensured Sharia principles, and enlarged the Two Holy Mosques.

King Abdullah (2005 – 2015)

Promoted Islamic solidarity, resolved Arab conflicts, supported international organizations, and advanced education and science.

King Salman (2015 – Present)

Launched Vision 2030, empowered women, and developed projects in transport, energy, and technology while leading the Kingdom forward.
